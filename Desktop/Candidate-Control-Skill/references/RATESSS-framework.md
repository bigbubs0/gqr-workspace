# RATESSS Framework
## Post-Interview Debrief Methodology

The RATESSS framework ensures systematic collection of candidate feedback after interviews to assess interest level, address concerns, and prepare effective client communication.

---

## Framework Overview

**RATESSS** is an acronym representing seven critical post-interview data collection areas:

- **R** - Relationships Formed
- **A** - Alternatives & Other Opportunities
- **T** - Timeline Updates
- **E** - Expectations (Changed or Confirmed)
- **S** - Sign Off & Buy-In Level
- **S** - Sell Points (What Impressed Them)
- **S** - Steps & Strategy Going Forward

---

## TIMING & APPROACH

### When to Conduct RATESSS Debrief

**Ideal Timing:**
- Within **2 hours** of the interview ending
- Same day whenever possible
- Before candidate's emotions and memories fade

**Why Timing Matters:**
- Captures authentic, immediate reactions
- Prevents candidate from overthinking or creating distance
- Maintains momentum and engagement
- Shows responsiveness and care

### Conversation Approach

**Opening:**
- Call with genuine enthusiasm: "I'd love to hear how it went!"
- Lead with an open-ended question
- Don't interrogate - have a conversation

**Throughout:**
- Let them talk **75% of the time**
- Listen actively for what they're NOT saying
- Probe deeper when answers are surface-level
- Take detailed notes verbatim for quotes
- Note tone and energy level

**Closing:**
- Reinforce their positives
- Address any concerns
- Confirm next steps
- Maintain enthusiasm

## R - RELATIONSHIPS FORMED

### Strategic Purpose
Assess chemistry with the team, identify champions vs. concerns, and understand interpersonal fit.

### Key Questions

**General:**
- "Walk me through who you met with today."
- "How did you connect with each person?"

**Specific Interviewers:**
- "Tell me about your conversation with [Interviewer Name]."
- "Did anyone particularly stand out to you, and why?"
- "Was there anyone you didn't connect with as well?"

**Team Dynamics:**
- "How did you feel about the team overall?"
- "Could you see yourself working closely with this group?"
- "What was the vibe/energy like?"

**Culture Observations:**
- "What did you notice about the company culture?"
- "How did people interact with each other?"

**Concerns:**
- "Was there anyone or anything that gave you pause?"
- "Did you sense any red flags in the team dynamic?"

### Data to Capture

- [ ] Names and titles of all interviewers
- [ ] Specific feedback on each person
- [ ] Standout positive connections
- [ ] Any poor chemistry or concerns
- [ ] Overall team impressions
- [ ] Culture observations
- [ ] Interpersonal fit assessment
- [ ] Potential champion identified
- [ ] Any relationship red flags

### What to Listen For

**Positive Signals:**
- Enthusiasm when describing specific people
- "I really connected with..."
- "They reminded me of..."
- Details about personal rapport
- Excitement about working with team
- Specific examples of good interactions

**Concerning Signals:**
- Vague descriptions of people
- "They were fine, I guess"
- Avoidance of discussing certain interviewers
- Concerns about team size/experience
- Lack of chemistry mentions
- Negative body language or tone
- "I'm not sure if..."

### Follow-Up Probes

If answers are vague:
- "What specifically about [person] resonated with you?"
- "Help me understand what you mean by [comment]."
- "Who do you think you'd work with most closely?"

If there are concerns:
- "Tell me more about that."
- "What made you feel that way?"
- "Is that a significant concern, or minor?"

## A - ALTERNATIVES & OTHER OPPORTUNITIES

### Strategic Purpose
Update competitive landscape, understand urgency, and assess where this opportunity now ranks.

### Key Questions

**Updates on Other Processes:**
- "Has anything changed with your other interview processes since we last talked?"
- "Any new interviews scheduled?"
- "Any offers received or expected?"

**Current Standing:**
- "Walk me through the status of each opportunity."
- "Which companies are moving fastest?"
- "When do you expect decisions from each?"

**Ranking:**
- "Now that you've interviewed here, how does this opportunity compare to the others?"
- "What's your ranking right now?"
- "Has this interview changed your ranking?"

**Competitive Intelligence:**
- "What are the other companies doing well in their process?"
- "How does our interview process compare?"
- "What are they offering that's compelling?"

**Decision Influence:**
- "If you received offers from multiple companies, what would be the deciding factors?"
- "What would make you choose us over [competitor]?"

### Data to Capture

- [ ] Updates on all competing processes
- [ ] New interviews or opportunities
- [ ] Offers received or imminent
- [ ] Timeline for each competitor
- [ ] Current ranking of all opportunities
- [ ] Change in ranking after this interview
- [ ] What competitors are doing well
- [ ] Decision criteria
- [ ] What would make us #1
- [ ] Urgency level

### What to Listen For

**Positive Signals:**
- "This is now my top choice"
- "This interview moved you up significantly"
- Declining other interviews
- Pushing back other timelines
- Less enthusiasm about competitors
- Specific reasons we're preferred

**Concerning Signals:**
- Other opportunities ranked higher
- Offers expected imminently elsewhere
- Accepting more interviews
- Accelerating other processes
- Strong preference for competitor
- Vague about our ranking

### Follow-Up Probes

If other opportunities are threats:
- "What specifically makes [competitor] more appealing right now?"
- "If we could address [concern], how would that change your ranking?"
- "What would you need to hear from us to move us to #1?"

If timeline pressure exists:
- "When would you need to give [competitor] an answer?"
- "Would you be willing to ask them for more time?"
- "What's our timeline to stay competitive?"

## T - TIMELINE UPDATES

### Strategic Purpose
Understand urgency, identify timeline pressures, and determine how quickly we need to move.

### Key Questions

**Offer Timelines:**
- "Have any of your offer timelines changed?"
- "When do you now expect to receive offers from other companies?"
- "Which company might make an offer first?"

**Decision Timeline:**
- "If you received multiple offers, how much time would you need to decide?"
- "What's your ideal timeline for making a decision?"

**Start Date:**
- "Has your target start date changed at all?"
- "What's the earliest you could start now?"

**Urgency Factors:**
- "Is there anything creating urgency in your search?"
- "Are you feeling pressure to decide quickly?"
- "What's driving your timeline?"

**Our Timeline:**
- "Our client typically [describe timeline] - how does that work for you?"
- "If we were to move quickly, could you keep up?"

### Data to Capture

- [ ] Updated offer expectations from each competitor
- [ ] Which competitor might offer first
- [ ] Candidate's decision timeline
- [ ] Start date updates
- [ ] Urgency factors
- [ ] Time needed to evaluate offers
- [ ] Our timeline feasibility
- [ ] Any deadline pressures
- [ ] Factors creating urgency

### What to Listen For

**Positive Signals:**
- Flexible timeline
- No immediate pressure
- "I can wait to hear from you"
- Willing to extend other deadlines
- No urgency to decide

**Concerning Signals:**
- Offers expected within days
- Strict deadlines
- Pressure from other companies
- Limited decision time
- Can't wait for our process
- Need to decide by [soon date]

### Follow-Up Probes

If timelines are tight:
- "What would happen if [competitor] gives you an exploding offer?"
- "Would you wait for us before accepting?"
- "How can we stay in contention given these timelines?"

If there's pressure:
- "Where is the pressure coming from?"
- "What could extend your timeline?"
- "What would make you comfortable waiting longer?"

## E - EXPECTATIONS (Changed or Confirmed)

### Strategic Purpose
Identify any misalignment between expectations and reality, address concerns before they become objections.

### Key Questions

**Pre vs. Post Interview:**
- "Going into the interview, what were you expecting?"
- "How did the reality compare to your expectations?"
- "Did anything surprise you - positive or negative?"

**Role Clarity:**
- "Is the role what you thought it would be?"
- "Did you get clarity on anything that was unclear before?"
- "Are there aspects of the role you now understand differently?"

**Company Perception:**
- "How does the company compare to what you expected?"
- "Did anything about the company culture or stage surprise you?"

**Concerns or Disappointments:**
- "Was there anything that didn't meet your expectations?"
- "Did anything give you pause or concern?"
- "What questions do you still have?"

**Positive Confirmations:**
- "What confirmed this is the right fit for you?"
- "What aligned with what you were hoping for?"

### Data to Capture

- [ ] Pre-interview expectations
- [ ] How reality compared
- [ ] Positive surprises
- [ ] Negative surprises
- [ ] Role clarity or confusion
- [ ] Company perception changes
- [ ] Unmet expectations
- [ ] Confirmed fit factors
- [ ] New concerns emerged
- [ ] Lingering questions

### What to Listen For

**Positive Signals:**
- "It was even better than expected"
- "This confirmed my interest"
- "I was hoping for X, and it delivered"
- Clear understanding of role
- Excitement about surprises

**Concerning Signals:**
- "I thought the role would be more [X]"
- "The company seems earlier stage than I realized"
- Confusion about role scope
- Unmet expectations
- "I'm not sure about..."
- Questions about fundamentals

### Follow-Up Probes

If expectations weren't met:
- "Tell me more about what you were expecting."
- "What would need to be true for this to be the right fit?"
- "Is that a dealbreaker, or something you could work with?"

If there's confusion:
- "What specifically is unclear?"
- "What information would help clarify?"
- "Should we schedule a follow-up call to address that?"

## S - SIGN OFF & BUY-IN LEVEL

### Strategic Purpose
Assess genuine interest level, identify likelihood of accepting an offer, and understand conditions for closing.

### Key Questions

**Interest Level:**
- "On a scale of 1-10, how excited are you about this opportunity?"
- "Why that number and not higher/lower?"
- "What would make it a 10?"

**Hypothetical Offer:**
- "If they were to extend an offer at your target compensation, would you accept it?"
- "What would make this a definite yes for you?"
- "What could make you say no?"

**Commitment Test:**
- "Are you ready to move forward with next steps?"
- "If they wanted to move quickly, are you in?"
- "What would give you pause about accepting?"

**Conditions for Yes:**
- "What would need to be included in an offer for you to say yes?"
- "Beyond compensation, what else matters?"
- "Are there any contingencies we should know about?"

**Hesitations:**
- "Is there anything holding you back right now?"
- "What concerns do you have about moving forward?"
- "What would make you more confident?"

### Data to Capture

- [ ] Interest rating (1-10)
- [ ] Reasoning for rating
- [ ] What would increase rating
- [ ] Likelihood of accepting offer
- [ ] Conditions for acceptance
- [ ] Deal-breakers or hesitations
- [ ] Competing pull factors
- [ ] Confidence level
- [ ] Readiness to commit
- [ ] Contingencies

### What to Listen For

**Positive Signals:**
- Rating of 8-10
- "I would absolutely accept"
- No hesitations
- Enthusiastic tone
- Ready to move forward
- "This checks all my boxes"

**Concerning Signals:**
- Rating below 7
- "It depends on..."
- Multiple contingencies
- Hesitation in response
- "I need to think about it"
- Comparison to other opportunities
- Significant conditions

### Follow-Up Probes

If interest is lukewarm:
- "What would increase your excitement?"
- "What's preventing you from being more excited?"
- "Is this more about the role or something else?"

If there are conditions:
- "How important is [condition] to you?"
- "What if [condition] couldn't be met?"
- "Are these must-haves or preferences?"

## S - SELL POINTS (What Impressed Them)

### Strategic Purpose
Identify what resonated most to help position the opportunity effectively and reinforce strengths in client communication.

### Key Questions

**Overall Impressions:**
- "What impressed you most about the interview?"
- "What got you most excited?"
- "What stood out as unique or special?"

**Specific Highlights:**
- "Was there a particular moment or conversation that resonated?"
- "What did you hear that made you think 'this is it'?"

**Value Proposition:**
- "What do you see as the biggest opportunity here?"
- "Why this company over others?"
- "What makes this role compelling for where you are in your career?"

**Competitive Advantage:**
- "How does this compare to other opportunities you're considering?"
- "What does this company/role offer that others don't?"

**Personal Fit:**
- "What about this role feels like the right fit for you personally?"
- "How does this align with your career goals?"

### Data to Capture

- [ ] Top 3 things that impressed them
- [ ] Specific moments that resonated
- [ ] Unique value proposition identified
- [ ] Competitive advantages vs. others
- [ ] Why this role/company
- [ ] Personal fit factors
- [ ] Career alignment
- [ ] What got them excited
- [ ] Memorable quotes

### What to Listen For

**Strong Sell Points:**
- Specific, enthusiastic descriptions
- "I loved that..."
- "This is exactly what I'm looking for"
- Connections to personal goals
- Unique aspects they value
- Energy and excitement in voice

**Weak Sell Points:**
- Generic descriptions
- "It was fine"
- Difficulty articulating what stood out
- Focus on table stakes ("good people")
- Lack of enthusiasm

### Follow-Up Probes

For deeper understanding:
- "Tell me more about why that matters to you."
- "How does that fit with your career aspirations?"
- "What would it mean for you to have that opportunity?"

To reinforce positives:
- "That's a great point - have you seen that elsewhere?"
- "How important is that factor in your decision?"

## S - STEPS & STRATEGY GOING FORWARD

### Strategic Purpose
Establish clear next steps, maintain momentum, and ensure candidate stays engaged through the process.

### Key Questions

**Immediate Next Steps:**
- "What are you hoping happens next?"
- "What's your next step from here?"
- "What information do you still need?"

**Process Expectations:**
- "What would an ideal next conversation look like?"
- "Are there specific people you'd want to meet?"
- "What questions do you want answered before moving forward?"

**Support Needed:**
- "How can I help move this forward?"
- "What do you need from me?"
- "Is there anything you'd like me to clarify with the client?"

**Timeline:**
- "When are you hoping to hear feedback?"
- "What's the timeline that works for you?"

**Obstacles:**
- "Is there anything that could slow down or derail this process?"
- "What concerns do you have about next steps?"

**Commitment:**
- "If they want to move to the next round, are you definitely interested?"
- "Assuming positive feedback, you're good to proceed?"

### Data to Capture

- [ ] Candidate's desired next steps
- [ ] Information gaps to fill
- [ ] Additional questions to answer
- [ ] People they want to meet
- [ ] Support needed from recruiter
- [ ] Expected timeline for feedback
- [ ] Obstacles to moving forward
- [ ] Commitment to continuing
- [ ] Concerns about process
- [ ] Strategy for staying competitive

### What to Listen For

**Positive Signals:**
- Clear next steps articulated
- Eager for feedback
- Ready to proceed
- Few remaining questions
- "Let's move forward"
- Proactive about strategy

**Concerning Signals:**
- Uncertainty about next steps
- Many remaining questions
- Hesitation to commit to proceeding
- Obstacles identified
- "I need to think about it"
- Passive approach

### Follow-Up Probes

If there's hesitation:
- "What's making you uncertain?"
- "What would give you confidence to proceed?"
- "Is there something specific holding you back?"

If there are obstacles:
- "How significant is [obstacle]?"
- "What would it take to overcome that?"
- "Can we address that together?"

## GENERATING CLIENT FEEDBACK

### What to Include in Client Email

**Must Include:**
1. Overall positive impression
2. Specific feedback on interviewers by name
3. What impressed them most
4. Ranking vs. competitors (e.g., "you're the frontrunner")
5. Interest level confirmation
6. Next steps candidate expects

**Tone:**
- Reassuring and positive
- Specific and detailed
- Professional
- Confident in candidate's interest

### What to Flag Internally (Not in Client Email)

**Must Flag for AM:**
1. Any negative feedback or concerns
2. Hesitations about moving forward
3. Competing processes and urgency
4. Compensation concerns that emerged
5. Conditions for acceptance
6. Risk factors
7. Closing strategy recommendations

## BEST PRACTICES

### During the Debrief

1. **Call within 2 hours** - Don't let candidate cool off
2. **Lead with enthusiasm** - Match their energy
3. **Let them talk 75%** - You're listening, not interviewing
4. **Take verbatim notes** - Capture exact quotes for client email
5. **Probe deeper** - "Tell me more" is your best friend
6. **Listen for subtext** - What are they NOT saying?
7. **Address concerns** - Don't let them fester
8. **Reinforce positives** - Help them sell themselves
9. **Maintain momentum** - Keep them engaged

### After the Debrief

1. **Draft client email immediately** - While fresh
2. **Separate internal notes** - Flag risks for AM
3. **Share timeline expectations** - When will client respond?
4. **Schedule follow-up** - Stay in touch even if waiting
5. **Update tracking** - Document in your system
6. **Prepare for next stage** - What's the closing strategy?

---

## COMMON PITFALLS TO AVOID

**Don't:**
- Wait too long to call (loses authenticity)
- Accept vague answers without probing
- Skip questions because you're in a hurry
- Only ask about positives (need to surface concerns)
- Forget to take detailed notes
- Overpromise on timeline or outcomes
- Let candidate leave without clear next steps
- Send negative feedback to client (keep internal)
- Miss competitive intelligence
- Assume interest without confirming

**Do:**
- Call within 2 hours every time
- Ask follow-up questions
- Create space for them to share concerns
- Document everything for later reference
- Be realistic about process and timeline
- Confirm mutual excitement
- Establish clear next steps
- Generate separate internal vs. client notes
- Gather competitive intelligence
- Explicitly confirm buy-in level
