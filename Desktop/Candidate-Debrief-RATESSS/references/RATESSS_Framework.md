# RATESSS Framework: Complete Question Set and Elicitation Techniques

## Core Principle
**The candidate should spend 75% of the debrief communicating information to you.**

Use open-ended questions, active listening, and strategic probing to extract maximum information while maintaining candidate control.

---

## R – RELATIONSHIPS

### Objective
Gather comprehensive impressions of the role, company, and individuals the candidate met.

### Required Questions

#### Opening (Broad)
- "How was the position described to you?"
- "What are your overall impressions of the company?"
- "Tell me about your favorite aspects of the position."

#### Individual Impressions (Critical)
- "What did you think of [Hiring Manager Name]?"
- "How did you find [each person they met]?"
- "What stood out to you about the team?"

#### Company Culture
- "What was your impression of the work environment?"
- "How would you describe the company culture based on what you saw?"

### Elicitation Technique
- **Use open-ended questions exclusively** - avoid yes/no questions
- **Let silence work** - pause after they answer to encourage elaboration
- **Mirror their energy** - match their enthusiasm level to build rapport
- **Ask for specifics** - if they say "it was good," ask "What specifically made it good?"

### Data to Capture
- Overall enthusiasm level (1-10 scale internally)
- Specific positive comments about individuals
- Any concerns about team dynamics or culture
- Comparison to their current environment

---

## A – ALTERNATIVES

### Objective
Identify red flags, lingering questions, competing processes, and potential deal-breakers early.

### Required Questions

#### Red Flags and Concerns
- "Do you have any red flags or concerns about the position?"
- "Was there anything that gave you pause?"
- "What questions remain unanswered for you?"

#### Competing Processes
- "What other opportunities are you currently exploring?"
- "Where are you in the process with other firms?"
- "Do you have any pending offers or expected offers?"

#### Commitment Level
- "If you were to receive multiple offers, how would this opportunity rank?"
- "Is there anything that would prevent you from accepting an offer if one were extended?"

### Elicitation Technique
- **Use softeners before blunt questions**: "Let me ask..." or "If I may ask..."
- **Acknowledge before probing**: "I can understand you might be looking at other options..."
- **Apply sidestep and redirect** for minor chemistry issues (see Soft Rebuttals)
- **Normalize multiple processes**: "Most strong candidates are speaking with several firms..."

### Data to Capture
- List of competing firms and interview stages
- Specific concerns or objections (verbatim if possible)
- Any timeline pressure from other offers
- Hidden objections or hesitations

### Critical Warning Signs
- Vague answers about other processes (hiding information)
- Excessive concerns about minor issues
- Lack of questions (disinterest signal)
- Defensive posture when asked about alternatives

---

## T – TIMELINE

### Objective
Understand the progression of other interviews, availability for next steps, and realistic acceptance timeline.

### Required Questions

#### Other Process Timelines
- "When do you expect to hear back from [other company]?"
- "What's the timeline on your other processes?"
- "Do you have any trips or commitments that would affect your availability?"

#### Next Steps Availability
- "When would you be available for a second interview?"
- "If they wanted to move quickly, could you accommodate that?"
- "Are there any dates you're unavailable in the next few weeks?"

#### Acceptance Timeline
- "If an offer were extended, how much time would you need to make a decision?"
- "What's your current notice period?"
- "Would you be able to extend deadlines on other offers if necessary?"

### Elicitation Technique
- **Clarify commitment to flexibility** - get explicit agreement they'll work with your timeline
- **Create urgency without pressure** - "The client tends to move quickly with top candidates"
- **Lock in availability** - get specific dates, not vague "anytime"
- **Test willingness to prioritize** - gauge if they'll delay other processes

### Data to Capture
- Specific dates for other interview rounds
- Notice period at current employer
- Travel/vacation conflicts
- Competing offer deadlines
- Flexibility level (1-10 scale internally)

---

## E – EXPECTATIONS

### Objective
Clarify compensation expectations, bonus structure, title requirements, and ensure alignment with client's likely offer.

### Required Questions

#### Compensation
- "What are your total compensation expectations for this role?"
- "How do you break down base versus bonus in your thinking?"
- "What's your current all-in compensation?"

#### Bonus and Equity
- "What are your expectations around bonus structure?"
- "How important is equity/stock options to you?"
- "What vesting timeline would you expect?"

#### Title and Level
- "What title are you expecting?"
- "Would you consider a lateral move for the right opportunity?"
- "How important is the title versus the scope of responsibility?"

#### Custom Concerns
- **Address their previously stated concerns**: "You mentioned slow growth at your current firm - how important is rapid advancement here?"
- **Link to their motivations**: "Given your interest in [X], how does this role's [Y] aspect align?"

### Elicitation Technique
- **Anchor to their current situation** - use their current comp as baseline
- **Separate needs from wants** - "What's non-negotiable versus nice-to-have?"
- **Test flexibility on title** - use the McDonald's franchise analogy if needed (see Soft Rebuttals)
- **Quantify everything** - get specific numbers, not ranges

### Data to Capture
- Exact compensation expectations (base, bonus, equity)
- Current compensation breakdown
- Title requirements and flexibility
- Deal-breakers versus negotiable items
- Alignment with likely client offer (gap analysis)

### Red Flags
- Expectations significantly above client's range (>20% gap)
- Rigid title requirements
- Unrealistic bonus expectations
- Lack of knowledge about their current comp

---

## S – SIGN OFF

### Objective
Identify who else influences the candidate's decision and temperature-test their commitment level.

### Required Questions

#### Decision Influencers
- "Who else is involved in your career decisions?"
- "Will you be discussing this with a spouse/partner?"
- "Do you have mentors or advisors you consult on career moves?"

#### Family Considerations
- "How does your family feel about a potential move?"
- "Are there any family considerations that would affect your decision?"

#### The Temperature Test (Critical)
- **"If an offer were extended today that met your expectations, when could you start?"**

This question is designed to surface their true commitment level and any hidden objections.

### Elicitation Technique
- **Ask the key question directly** - don't soften or avoid it
- **Listen to hesitation** - pauses or vague answers indicate concerns
- **Probe delays** - if they say "I'd need to think about it," ask why
- **Identify influencers early** - you need to manage all stakeholders

### Data to Capture
- Names and relationships of decision influencers
- Spousal/partner support level
- Any family constraints (relocation, timing, etc.)
- Commitment level based on "when could you start" answer
- Hidden objections revealed by hesitation

### Response Analysis
- **Strong**: "I could give two weeks notice and start in three weeks"
- **Moderate**: "I'd need to discuss with my wife first"
- **Weak**: "I'm not sure, I'd have to think about several things"
- **Red Flag**: "That's premature, I'm still exploring options"

---

## S – SELL

### Objective
Reinforce the candidate's value and interest by amplifying positives through specific questioning.

### Required Questions

#### Reinforcing Positives
- "What specifically excited you most about the opportunity?"
- "You mentioned you liked [hiring manager] - what exactly about them impressed you?"
- "What aspects of the role align best with your career goals?"

#### Value Proposition
- "How do you see this role advancing your career?"
- "What skills would you be able to develop here?"
- "How does this compare to your ideal next move?"

### Elicitation Technique
- **Ask for specifics, not generalities** - "What exactly did you like?" not "Did you like it?"
- **Reinforce and expand** - when they mention a positive, agree and add to it
- **Connect to their motivations** - link their stated goals to role benefits
- **Build momentum** - string together positives to create enthusiasm

### Example Reinforcement Pattern
**Candidate**: "I liked the hiring manager."
**You**: "That's great to hear - [Hiring Manager] is extremely well-respected. What specifically impressed you about them?"
**Candidate**: "They seemed very knowledgeable about the industry."
**You**: "Absolutely, they've been in this space for 15 years and have incredible connections. What else stood out?"

### Data to Capture
- Specific positive comments (quote directly when possible)
- Enthusiasm indicators
- Alignment between role and candidate goals
- Selling points to emphasize in client feedback

---

## S – STEPS/STRATEGY

### Objective
Confirm interest in next steps, clarify how the interview concluded, and establish the follow-up strategy.

### Required Questions

#### Interest Confirmation
- "Are you interested in moving to the next stage?"
- "Would you like to proceed with a second interview if offered?"
- "On a scale of 1-10, how interested are you in this opportunity?"

#### Interview Conclusion
- "How was the interview left?"
- "Did they indicate next steps or timeline?"
- "What was the last thing discussed?"

#### Next Steps
- "What happens next from your perspective?"
- "When do you expect to hear back?"

### Mandatory Closing Statement
**"I'm going to contact the client and provide them with complete feedback - both positive and negative. I assume you'll want to know both as well."**

This statement:
- Sets expectation for honest two-way feedback
- Positions you as neutral intermediary
- Establishes that you'll follow up with full debrief
- Maintains professional control

### Elicitation Technique
- **Confirm explicit interest** - don't assume, get verbal confirmation
- **Clarify ambiguity** - if they're unsure, probe why
- **Set follow-up expectation** - tell them when you'll contact them again
- **Close with professionalism** - maintain measured, dignified tone

### Data to Capture
- Explicit interest level (1-10)
- Next steps as understood by candidate
- Client's indicated timeline
- Any commitments made by either party
- Candidate's availability for follow-up call

---

## Quality Control Checklist

After completing RATESSS debrief, verify:

- [ ] All seven elements addressed in order
- [ ] Candidate spoke 75%+ of the time
- [ ] Open-ended questions used throughout
- [ ] Specific examples captured, not generalities
- [ ] All competing processes and timelines documented
- [ ] Compensation expectations quantified
- [ ] Decision influencers identified
- [ ] Temperature test completed ("when could you start")
- [ ] Explicit interest in next steps confirmed
- [ ] Follow-up plan communicated

---

## Common Pitfalls to Avoid

1. **Asking yes/no questions** - Always ask open-ended
2. **Talking too much** - You should speak <25% of the time
3. **Skipping the temperature test** - Always ask "when could you start"
4. **Accepting vague answers** - Probe for specifics
5. **Avoiding difficult questions** - Use softeners but ask everything
6. **Failing to quantify** - Get numbers, dates, rankings
7. **Not capturing verbatim quotes** - Record exact positive comments
8. **Rushing through** - Let silence work, give them time to think
9. **Showing bias** - Remain neutral even if they express concerns
10. **Forgetting to sell** - Always reinforce positives before ending
