# Client Feedback Email Template
## Format and Examples for Post-Interview Communication

This template provides the exact structure for client feedback emails generated after RATESSS debrief.

---

## TEMPLATE STRUCTURE

### Subject Line Format
```
Interview Debrief - [Candidate Name]
```

**Examples:**
- "Interview Debrief - Sarah Chen"
- "Interview Feedback - Michael Rodriguez"
- "Post-Interview Update - Jennifer Park"

---

## BLACK TEXT SECTION (Client-Facing)

### Paragraph 1: Overall Impression & Enthusiasm
Open with the candidate's overall positive sentiment and excitement level.

**Template:**
```
I just spoke with [Candidate Name] about [yesterday's/this morning's] interview, and [he/she/they] [was/were] [positive sentiment descriptor]. [Brief statement about what impressed them most or their overall takeaway].
```

**Examples:**
```
I just spoke with Sarah about yesterday's interview, and she was genuinely enthusiastic about the conversation. She was particularly impressed by the strategic vision you outlined for the product roadmap and felt there was strong alignment with her experience in scaling enterprise SaaS products.
```

```
I connected with Michael this morning to debrief his interview, and he's very excited about the opportunity. He was energized by the technical depth of the conversation and the collaborative approach he experienced with your engineering team.
```

---

### Paragraph 2: Specific Feedback & Interviewer Impressions
Provide detailed feedback on the interview experience and specific people they met.

**Template:**
```
[Candidate Name] specifically mentioned [his/her/their] conversation with [Interviewer Name and Title], noting that [he/she/they] [specific positive feedback]. [Additional feedback about other interviewers or aspects of the interview]. [What stood out about the team, culture, or company].
```

**Example:**
```
Sarah specifically mentioned her conversation with Mark Chen, your VP of Engineering, noting that she appreciated his collaborative approach and the technical depth of their discussion about scaling architecture. She also enjoyed speaking with Jennifer Wu from the founding team and was energized by the mission-driven focus and company culture she observed throughout her conversations. She felt the team dynamic was exactly what she's been looking for - technically strong, execution-focused, and genuinely collaborative.
```

---

### Paragraph 3: Competitive Positioning & Interest Level
Clearly state where this opportunity ranks among their alternatives and their level of interest.

**Template:**
```
In terms of where this opportunity stands among [his/her/their] other options, [he/she/they] indicated that [your company/the role/this opportunity] [ranking statement]. [Context about competing processes if relevant]. [Clear statement of continued/strong interest].
```

**Examples:**

**When you're the frontrunner:**
```
In terms of where this stands among her other opportunities, Sarah indicated that your company has moved to the top of her list. She's in late stages with two other companies and expects to receive an offer from one by November 15th, but she was clear that if you move forward, this would be her preferred choice.
```

**When you're competitive but not #1:**
```
In terms of her other opportunities, Sarah shared that she's evaluating this alongside one other final-stage process. Both companies represent strong fits for different reasons, and she's eager to understand your feedback and timeline to help inform her decision. She emphasized that the mission alignment and team dynamic here are exactly what she's looking for.
```

**When timeline pressure exists:**
```
With regard to his timeline, Michael mentioned he has an offer from another company with a deadline of November 20th. However, he was explicit that he would very much like to hear your feedback before making any decisions, and he's hoping to understand your timeline and next steps.
```

---

### Paragraph 4: Next Steps & Closing
Confirm what the candidate is hoping for and invite client's feedback.

**Template:**
```
[He/She/They] [is/are] eager to hear your feedback and [discuss next steps/learn about your timeline/understand your thoughts]. Please let me know your impressions when you have a chance.
```

**Examples:**
```
She's very eager to hear your feedback and discuss potential next steps. Please let me know your thoughts when you have a chance.
```

```
He's looking forward to hearing your impressions and understanding your timeline for making a decision. Please share your feedback when convenient.
```

## RED TEXT SECTION (Internal Feedback Notes)

### Required Components

Use this exact format:
```
🔴 INTERNAL FEEDBACK - DO NOT SHARE WITH CLIENT

[Organized sections as shown below]
```

---

### Component 1: Concerns Raised
```
CONCERNS RAISED:
- [Specific concern about role, company, or interview]
- [Additional concerns]
- [Context for each concern]
```

**Example:**
```
CONCERNS RAISED:
- Team is smaller than expected (3 PMs total vs. 5+ she anticipated)
- Uncertain about budget for product initiatives and engineering resources
- Wants clearer reporting structure - currently reports to CEO, would prefer VP of Product already in place
- Office location is slightly farther commute than ideal (45 min vs. 30 min preferred)
- Concerned about runway and next fundraise timeline (wants 18+ months confirmed)
```

---

### Component 2: Compensation Discussion Updates
```
COMPENSATION DISCUSSION:
- [Any changes to compensation expectations]
- [Competing offers or ranges discussed]
- [Leverage points or concerns]
- [Recommendations for positioning]
```

**Example:**
```
COMPENSATION DISCUSSION:
- After interview, signaled she may push for higher end of range ($230K vs. $220K discussed)
- Mentioned DataCorp will likely offer $240K base (our client's range is $200-230K)
- Will need strong equity story to offset cash difference
- Asked specifically about equity acceleration clauses (concerned about early-stage risk)
- Also asked about refresh grant cadence and how performance affects equity awards
```

---

### Component 3: Ranking Update
```
RANKING UPDATE:
- [Current ranking vs. before interview]
- [What moved]
- [What would make them #1]
- [Threats from competitors]
```

**Example:**
```
RANKING UPDATE:
- This moved from #2 to #1 after the interview (was previously behind DataCorp)
- Interview experience and team chemistry were significantly better than expected
- DataCorp still a threat primarily due to compensation (they'll offer ~$40K more in cash)
- Would choose us if compensation was within 10% of DataCorp
- Founder/CEO conversation was the turning point - really connected with vision
```

---

### Component 4: Urgency & Timeline
```
URGENCY & TIMELINE:
- [Competing offer deadlines]
- [Our timeline requirements]
- [Critical dates]
- [Timing strategy]
```

**Example:**
```
URGENCY & TIMELINE:
- DataCorp offer expected November 15th (6 days from now)
- Will need 3-4 days to evaluate any offer and discuss with spouse
- Our timeline needs to be: feedback by 11/11, offer by 11/14 to stay competitive
- If we wait past 11/14, she'll likely accept DataCorp offer
- She's willing to ask DataCorp for extension if we can commit to moving quickly
```

---

### Component 5: Buy-In Level Assessment
```
BUY-IN LEVEL:
- [Interest rating if applicable]
- [Likelihood of accepting]
- [Conditions for acceptance]
- [Hesitation factors]
```

**Example:**
```
BUY-IN LEVEL:
- Rated interest 8/10 (was 6/10 before interview)
- Would accept at $230K base + strong equity package (4-year vest, 1-year cliff, standard terms)
- Key conditions:
  * Clear path to VP Product within 18 months
  * Team growth plan (wants to hire 2 more PMs in first year)
  * Runway confirmed at 18+ months
- At $220K base, would need exceptional equity or other benefits to accept
```

---

### Component 6: Specific Hesitations
```

HESITATIONS:
- [Concerns about moving forward]
- [Doubts or reservations]
- [What's making them uncertain]
```

**Example:**
```
HESITATIONS:
- Company stage (Series A) makes her nervous about stability and resources
- Wants to see detailed cap table and understand dilution risk
- Concerned about level of autonomy - CEO seems very involved in product decisions
- Worried about salary decrease from current role (even at $230K)
- Spouse is risk-averse and pushing her toward more established company (DataCorp)
- Wants customer references to validate product-market fit (unusual request - flag for client)
```

---

### Component 7: Additional Information Needed
```
NEXT STEPS / INFORMATION NEEDED:
- [What candidate wants to see/hear/understand]
- [Additional meetings requested]
- [Questions to answer]
- [Materials to provide]
```

**Example:**
```
NEXT STEPS / INFORMATION NEEDED:
- Wants to meet with one more founding team member (CTO) to discuss technical vision
- Requested detailed product roadmap for next 12 months
- Asked for customer references (2-3 customers willing to speak about product)
- Wants cap table summary (basic dilution understanding)
- Needs clarity on reporting structure and when VP Product might be hired
- Asked about benefits package details (health insurance, 401k match, etc.)
```

---

### Component 8: Closing Strategy & Recommendations
```
CLOSING STRATEGY:
- [Recommendations for next steps]
- [How to position offer]
- [What to emphasize]
- [What to address]
- [Timeline recommendations]
```

**Example:**
```
CLOSING STRATEGY:
- Schedule CEO follow-up call ASAP to discuss VP path and product autonomy
- Prepare equity comparison model showing total comp scenarios (vs. DataCorp $240K + minimal equity)
- Move to offer by 11/14 at the latest to stay competitive
- Consider offering $225K base + enhanced equity package (more options or acceleration clause) to split the difference
- Emphasize growth opportunity and impact vs. bureaucracy at established company
- Address runway/fundraising concerns directly (provide specifics on runway and customer traction)
- Provide 2-3 customer references proactively
- Consider offering sign-on bonus to offset salary decrease and DataCorp cash advantage
```

## VARIATIONS FOR DIFFERENT SCENARIOS

### When Interest is Lower Than Expected

```
While Sarah enjoyed meeting the team and learning more about the company, I sensed her enthusiasm was more measured than I had anticipated. She appreciated the mission and the technical challenges, but had some questions about the stage of the company and the team structure that I think would benefit from additional conversation.

[Continue with specifics, but be honest in internal notes about lower-than-expected interest]

🔴 INTERNAL: Interest level dropped from 7/10 to 5/10 after interview. Primary concerns are [list]. Recommend addressing these before moving forward or considering if this is the right fit.
```

---

### When There Are Significant Red Flags

```
I debriefed with Sarah this morning about the interview. She shared positive feedback about [specific positives], and I want to make sure you have her full perspective as you evaluate next steps.

[Be measured in client communication, flag concerns appropriately in internal notes]

🔴 INTERNAL: SIGNIFICANT CONCERNS - [List major red flags]. Recommend discussing internally before proceeding. She may not be the right fit because [reasons].
```

---

### When Candidate is Lukewarm But Salvageable

```
Sarah and I just connected about yesterday's interview. She appreciated the opportunity to learn more about the role and the team. She had good conversations with [names] and is interested in understanding your timeline and next steps.

[Keep it neutral but positive in client communication]

🔴 INTERNAL: Interest is lukewarm (5/10). She's not sold yet but salvageable. Key issues: [list]. To move forward, we need to [actions]. If client is very interested, we can work to build her excitement, but it will require addressing [concerns] directly.
```

## COMMUNICATION PRINCIPLES

### Be Truthful But Positive
- Don't hide concerns from the AM
- Frame positively for the client
- Be honest about interest level

### Be Specific
- Use real quotes when possible
- Name specific people and interactions
- Provide concrete examples

### Be Strategic
- Help the client understand competitive landscape
- Provide actionable closing recommendations
- Flag urgency appropriately

### Be Professional
- Maintain confidence in candidate
- Present balanced perspective
- Focus on solutions, not just problems
