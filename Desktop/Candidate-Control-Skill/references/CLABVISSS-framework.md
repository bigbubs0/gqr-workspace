# CLABVISSS Framework
## Comprehensive Candidate Intake Methodology

The CLABVISSS framework ensures systematic collection of all critical candidate data points before client submission. Each component serves a specific strategic purpose in maintaining candidate control and assessing fit.

---

## Framework Overview

**CLABVISSS** is an acronym representing nine critical data collection areas:

- **C** - Commitment & Flexibility
- **L** - Location Preferences
- **A** - Availability & Timeline
- **B** - Background Verification
- **V** - Visa & Work Authorization
- **I** - Ideal vs. Acceptable Role
- **S** - Salary & Compensation
- **S** - Start Date Flexibility
- **S** - Stage in Other Processes

---

## C - COMMITMENT & FLEXIBILITY

### Strategic Purpose
Assess the candidate's genuine commitment to the search process and willingness to prioritize this opportunity over competing options.

### Key Questions

**Opening (Conversational):**
- "I'd like to understand where you are in your job search - are you actively interviewing right now?"

**Commitment Level:**
- "If we were to move quickly and our client wanted to present an offer within the next two weeks, would you be prepared to make a decision?"
- "What would you need to feel comfortable making a decision on that timeline?"

**Priority Assessment:**
- "If you receive an offer from another company first, would you be willing to extend your decision deadline to hear from us?"
- "How do you typically approach making career decisions - do you like to move quickly or take your time?"

**Flexibility Exploration:**
- "Are you open to adjusting your timeline based on the right opportunity?"
- "What factors would make you prioritize one opportunity over another?"

### Data to Capture

- [ ] Current search status (active, passive, casual)
- [ ] Number of active processes
- [ ] Willingness to move quickly
- [ ] Deadline extension flexibility
- [ ] Decision-making style
- [ ] Factors influencing prioritization
- [ ] Level of genuine interest vs. "just exploring"

### Red Flags to Watch For

- Unwilling to extend deadlines for competing offers
- Vague about commitment level
- "Just seeing what's out there" mentality
- Inflexible timeline demands
- Indication that another opportunity is strongly preferred

### Follow-Up Probes

If answers are vague:
- "Can you help me understand what that would look like in practice?"
- "What concerns do you have about moving quickly?"
- "What would need to be true for you to feel comfortable committing?"

## L - LOCATION PREFERENCES

### Strategic Purpose
Identify any geographic constraints that could derail the process and assess cultural fit regarding remote/hybrid work.

### Key Questions

**Current Situation:**
- "Where are you currently based?"
- "Are you happy with your current location?"

**Role Location:**
- "This role is [location/remote/hybrid] - how does that fit with what you're looking for?"
- "What's your ideal work setup in terms of location and remote flexibility?"

**Relocation Assessment:**
- "If relocation were required, would that be something you'd consider?"
- "Have you relocated for work before? What was that experience like?"

**Commute & Logistics:**
- "If this role requires time in the office, what's your commute situation?"
- "Are there any geographic constraints we should be aware of?"

**Family Considerations:**
- "Do you have any family or personal considerations that affect your location flexibility?"

### Data to Capture

- [ ] Current location
- [ ] Commute tolerance
- [ ] Relocation willingness and timeline
- [ ] Remote work expectations/requirements
- [ ] Hybrid schedule preferences
- [ ] Family/partner location constraints
- [ ] Geographic deal-breakers

### Red Flags to Watch For

- Strong preference that conflicts with role requirements
- Unrealistic remote work expectations
- Unwillingness to relocate when required
- Partner/family constraints not initially disclosed
- Long commute concerns

### Follow-Up Probes

If answers are vague:
- "What would make relocation feel worth it to you?"
- "How does your family feel about potential relocation?"
- "What's your ideal commute time?"

## A - AVAILABILITY & TIMELINE

### Strategic Purpose
Understand the candidate's urgency, competing timelines, and ability to start quickly if needed.

### Key Questions

**Current Stage:**
- "Walk me through where you are with each company you're interviewing with."
- "What stage are you at in each process?"

**Offer Timelines:**
- "When do you expect to receive offers from the companies you're interviewing with?"
- "Are any of these on a faster timeline than others?"

**Decision Timeline:**
- "If everything aligned perfectly, when would you ideally want to start a new role?"
- "What's driving that timeline?"

**Notice Period:**
- "What's your notice period at your current company?"
- "Have you given any indication to your employer that you're looking?"

**Start Date:**
- "If you received an offer you were excited about, what's the earliest you could realistically start?"
- "Are there any factors that could delay your start date - projects, bonuses, equity vesting?"

### Data to Capture

- [ ] List of active interviews with companies
- [ ] Stage in each process
- [ ] Expected offer dates from each
- [ ] Fastest-moving competitor
- [ ] Ideal start date
- [ ] Notice period length
- [ ] Earliest possible start date
- [ ] Factors affecting timeline (bonus cycles, project commitments, equity vesting)

### Red Flags to Watch For

- Multiple offers expected imminently
- Inflexible start date requirements
- Long notice period (60+ days)
- Competing process significantly ahead
- Vesting/bonus timing that conflicts with start date

### Follow-Up Probes

If answers are vague:
- "Help me understand the specifics - when exactly is your next interview with Company X?"
- "If they made you an offer today, when would you need to respond by?"
- "What would happen if you needed to start two weeks earlier than planned?"

## B - BACKGROUND VERIFICATION

### Strategic Purpose
Identify any potential issues that could surface during background checks or reference calls, avoiding surprises late in the process.

### Key Questions

**Employment History:**
- "Is your resume a complete picture of your work history?"
- "Are there any gaps in employment we should be aware of?"

**Reference Checks:**
- "Who would you provide as references from your recent roles?"
- "Is there anyone from your work history we should avoid contacting, and why?"

**Performance History:**
- "Have you ever been on a performance improvement plan?"
- "Have you ever left a role under less-than-ideal circumstances?"

**Credentials:**
- "Do you have all the certifications and degrees listed on your resume?"
- "Are there any credential verifications that might be complicated?"

**Non-Compete/Legal:**
- "Are you under any non-compete or non-solicitation agreements?"
- "Are there any legal considerations from previous employers we should know about?"

### Data to Capture

- [ ] Employment gaps and explanations
- [ ] Reference availability and quality
- [ ] Any problematic departures
- [ ] Performance issues in past roles
- [ ] Credential verification concerns
- [ ] Non-compete/legal restrictions
- [ ] Anything that would raise questions in background check

### Red Flags to Watch For

- Unexplained employment gaps
- Reluctance to provide references
- Vague about reasons for leaving roles
- Multiple short tenures
- Credential concerns
- Active non-compete agreements
- Legal issues with previous employers

### Follow-Up Probes

If answers are vague or concerning:
- "Can you walk me through what happened there?"
- "How would your former manager describe your time there?"
- "What would come up if we spoke to your references about that situation?"

## V - VISA & WORK AUTHORIZATION

### Strategic Purpose
Identify work authorization issues early to avoid wasted time on candidates who can't legally accept the role.

### Key Questions

**Current Status:**
- "What's your current work authorization status in [country]?"
- "Are you authorized to work without sponsorship?"

**Sponsorship Needs:**
- "Do you now or will you in the future require visa sponsorship?"
- "What type of visa are you currently on?"

**Timing & Expiration:**
- "When does your current work authorization expire?"
- "Are you in the process of renewing or changing your status?"

**Transfer Process:**
- "If you're on a visa, how long does it typically take to transfer to a new employer?"
- "Have you transferred sponsorship before? Any complications?"

**Long-Term Status:**
- "Are you in the process of obtaining permanent residency or citizenship?"
- "What's your timeline for that?"

**Travel Restrictions:**
- "Are there any travel restrictions we should be aware of?"

### Data to Capture

- [ ] Current authorization status
- [ ] Visa type (H-1B, L-1, TN, Green Card, etc.)
- [ ] Sponsorship requirements (now and future)
- [ ] Expiration dates
- [ ] Transfer timeline and process
- [ ] Path to permanent status
- [ ] Travel restrictions
- [ ] Any complications or pending status changes

### Red Flags to Watch For

- Requires immediate sponsorship
- Visa expiring soon
- Complicated immigration status
- Limited transfer window
- Travel restrictions affecting role
- Client doesn't sponsor visas
- Cap-subject H-1B issues

### Follow-Up Probes

If answers are uncertain:
- "Have you consulted with an immigration attorney about this?"
- "What would happen if you needed to start before the transfer is complete?"
- "Are there any scenarios where your authorization could be denied?"

## I - IDEAL ROLE vs. ACCEPTABLE MINIMUM

### Strategic Purpose
Understand the candidate's flexibility and identify what they truly need versus what they'd "like to have" to avoid misalignment later.

### Key Questions

**Ideal Scenario:**
- "If you could design your perfect next role, what would it look like?"
- "What responsibilities and scope would be ideal?"
- "What type of team and company culture fits you best?"

**Must-Haves:**
- "What are the absolute must-haves - things that if missing, would be a dealbreaker?"
- "What's non-negotiable for you?"

**Nice-to-Haves:**
- "What would be nice to have but isn't required?"
- "Where are you flexible?"

**Acceptable Minimum:**
- "If the ideal isn't available, what would still be 'good enough' for you to make a move?"
- "What's the minimum scope or level you'd consider?"

**Deal-Breakers:**
- "What would immediately make you lose interest in a role?"
- "What have you turned down in the past, and why?"

### Data to Capture

- [ ] Ideal role description
- [ ] Responsibilities and scope desired
- [ ] Must-have requirements
- [ ] Nice-to-have preferences
- [ ] Acceptable minimum standards
- [ ] Flexibility areas
- [ ] Deal-breakers
- [ ] Past turndowns and reasons

### Red Flags to Watch For

- Unrealistic expectations for career stage
- Inflexible on minor details
- "Ideal" doesn't match market reality
- "Acceptable minimum" is above role scope
- Multiple deal-breakers
- Recently turned down similar roles

### Follow-Up Probes

If answers are unrealistic:
- "Help me understand what would make that feasible at this stage of your career."
- "Have you seen roles at your level with that scope?"
- "What trade-offs would you be willing to make?"

## S - SALARY & COMPENSATION EXPECTATIONS

### Strategic Purpose
Establish clear compensation expectations and context to avoid misalignment and enable effective negotiation later.

### Key Questions

**Current Compensation:**
- "Can you break down your current total compensation package?"
- "What's your base salary?"
- "What's your target bonus, and do you typically hit it?"
- "Do you receive equity? What's the current/expected value?"
- "Any other compensation - car allowance, 401k match, etc.?"

**Target Compensation:**
- "What are you targeting for total compensation in your next role?"
- "How did you arrive at that number?"
- "Is that number flexible depending on the opportunity?"

**Base vs. Total:**
- "How do you weight base salary versus total comp?"
- "Would you consider a lower base for significant equity upside?"

**Minimum Acceptable:**
- "What's the minimum offer you'd consider accepting?"
- "At what point would compensation keep you from accepting an otherwise great role?"

**Compensation Motivations:**
- "Beyond the number itself, what's important to you about compensation?"
- "Are you motivated more by cash now or long-term wealth building?"

**Market Context:**
- "Have you researched market rates for this type of role?"
- "Have other companies indicated compensation ranges?"

### Data to Capture

- [ ] Current base salary
- [ ] Current bonus (target and actual)
- [ ] Current equity value (vested and unvested)
- [ ] Other benefits/perks with monetary value
- [ ] Total current compensation
- [ ] Target total compensation
- [ ] Minimum acceptable offer
- [ ] Base salary minimum
- [ ] Equity vs. cash preference
- [ ] Compensation motivations
- [ ] Market awareness
- [ ] Competing offers or ranges discussed

### Red Flags to Watch For

- Expectations significantly above market
- Inflexible on compensation structure
- Unrealistic equity expectations
- Unwilling to discuss minimums
- Conflicting numbers across conversations
- No market research done
- Purely money-motivated

### Follow-Up Probes

If answers are vague or concerning:
- "Can you help me understand how you arrived at that target?"
- "What would make you comfortable with a lower number?"
- "If we were 10% below your target but had strong equity, would that work?"
- "Have you received offers at that level before?"

## S - START DATE FLEXIBILITY

### Strategic Purpose
Assess the candidate's ability to accommodate accelerated timelines if the client wants to move quickly.

### Key Questions

**Ideal Start Date:**
- "When would you ideally like to start a new role?"
- "What's driving that timeline?"

**Standard Notice:**
- "What's your standard notice period?"
- "Is it strictly enforced at your company?"

**Early Start Possibility:**
- "If an amazing opportunity required you to start two weeks earlier than planned, could you make that work?"
- "What would you need to do to accommodate an earlier start?"

**Obstacles:**
- "What could prevent you from starting quickly?"
- "Are there projects, transitions, or commitments that affect your timeline?"

**Negotiation Ability:**
- "Have you ever negotiated an early release from notice?"
- "How flexible is your current employer?"

**Worst Case:**
- "What's the latest you could start before the opportunity loses its appeal?"

### Data to Capture

- [ ] Ideal start date
- [ ] Standard notice period
- [ ] Notice period enforcement
- [ ] Earliest possible start date
- [ ] Factors affecting timeline
- [ ] Flexibility level (high, medium, low)
- [ ] Current employer's likely response
- [ ] Projects or commitments creating constraints
- [ ] Latest acceptable start date

### Red Flags to Watch For

- Rigid notice period requirements
- Critical projects extending timeline
- Multiple timing constraints
- Unwillingness to negotiate early start
- Start date months away
- Notice period longer than 4 weeks

### Follow-Up Probes

If flexibility is limited:
- "What would make an earlier start worth the effort?"
- "Have others at your company left with shorter notice?"
- "What's the worst that could happen if you gave shorter notice?"

## S - STAGE IN OTHER PROCESSES

### Strategic Purpose
Understand competitive landscape, timing pressures, and the candidate's true level of interest relative to other opportunities.

### Key Questions

**Current Processes:**
- "Can you walk me through every company you're currently interviewing with?"
- "For each one, where are you in their process?"

**Process Details:**
- "When is your next interview with [Company X]?"
- "When do you expect to receive an offer from them?"
- "How many rounds do they typically have?"

**Ranking:**
- "How would you rank these opportunities in terms of your excitement?"
- "Which one is the frontrunner right now?"
- "What makes [frontrunner] more appealing?"

**Decision Factors:**
- "If you received offers from all these companies at similar compensation, how would you decide?"
- "What are the key differentiators for you?"

**Our Standing:**
- "Where does our opportunity rank in comparison?"
- "What would move us to #1?"

**Updates:**
- "How often should I expect your list to change?"
- "Are you planning to start any new interview processes?"

### Data to Capture

- [ ] Complete list of active interviews
- [ ] Company names
- [ ] Interview stages (phone screen, first round, final, offer expected, etc.)
- [ ] Interview dates scheduled
- [ ] Expected offer timelines
- [ ] Frontrunner identification
- [ ] Ranking of all opportunities (including ours)
- [ ] Decision criteria
- [ ] What would change the ranking
- [ ] Likelihood of new processes starting

### Red Flags to Watch For

- Many processes significantly ahead
- Multiple offers expected imminently
- We're ranked low vs. competitors
- Vague about specific competitors
- Adding new processes constantly
- Clear frontrunner that isn't us
- Decision factors don't align with our role

### Follow-Up Probes

If ranking is concerning:
- "What specifically makes [competitor] more appealing?"
- "If we addressed [concern], how would that change things?"
- "What have they done differently in their process?"

If timelines are tight:
- "If Company X makes you an offer next week, what would you do?"
- "How much time would you need to make a decision?"
- "Would you wait to hear from us before deciding?"

## INTAKE COMPLETION CHECKLIST

Before moving to CV Submission phase, confirm:

### Data Completeness
- [ ] All 9 CLABVISSS components captured
- [ ] Specific details provided (no vague "I'm flexible" answers)
- [ ] Follow-up questions asked where needed
- [ ] Numerical data where applicable (salary figures, dates, etc.)

### Red Flags Identified
- [ ] Visa/authorization concerns
- [ ] Compensation misalignment
- [ ] Competing offer risks
- [ ] Flexibility/commitment concerns
- [ ] Any deal-breakers or obstacles

### Interest Assessment
- [ ] Genuine interest level evaluated
- [ ] Ranking vs. competitors understood
- [ ] Motivation for move is clear
- [ ] Candidate engagement was strong

### Internal Confidence
- [ ] Recruiter feels confident in candidate's fit
- [ ] Timeline is realistic and manageable
- [ ] No major surprises likely
- [ ] Ready to present to client

## BEST PRACTICES

### During the Intake Conversation

1. **One question at a time** - Don't overwhelm the candidate
2. **Listen more than you talk** - Candidate should speak 75% of the time
3. **Probe for specifics** - "I'm flexible" is not an answer
4. **Acknowledge responses** - Validate before moving to next question
5. **Take detailed notes** - You'll need this data for CV submission
6. **Note exact quotes** - Especially for motivations and selling points
7. **Watch for red flags** - Note concerns as they arise
8. **Maintain conversational tone** - Not interrogational
9. **Build rapport** - This strengthens candidate control

### After the Intake

1. **Review notes immediately** - Fill in gaps while fresh
2. **Flag concerns for AM** - Don't minimize red flags
3. **Assess submission readiness** - Are we confident in this candidate?
4. **Verify completeness** - Do we have everything we need?
5. **Prepare for CV submission** - Organize data for email generation
