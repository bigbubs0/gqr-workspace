# CV Submission Email Template
## Format and Examples for Account Manager Review

This template provides the exact structure for CV submission emails generated after CLABVISSS intake.

---

## TEMPLATE STRUCTURE

### Subject Line Format
```
[Candidate Name] - [X Years] of [Specific Experience Type]
```

**Examples:**
- "Sarah Chen - 8 Years Enterprise SaaS Product Management"
- "Michael Rodriguez - 12 Years Software Engineering Leadership"
- "Jennifer Park - 6 Years Digital Marketing & Growth Strategy"

---

## BLACK TEXT SECTION (Client-Facing)

### Paragraph 1: Introduction & Experience Summary
State who they are, years of experience, and 2-3 compelling selling points relevant to the role.

**Template:**
```
[Candidate Name] brings [X years] of [specific experience type] with [key differentiator or achievement]. [Brief description of current role and its scope]. [1-2 sentences highlighting relevant achievements with numbers/metrics].
```

**Example:**
```
Sarah Chen brings 8 years of enterprise SaaS product management experience with a proven track record of scaling products from early stage to $50M+ ARR. She currently leads a team of 5 at TechCorp, managing a product line that has consistently delivered 20% year-over-year revenue growth. Before TechCorp, she was instrumental in bringing a B2B analytics platform from beta to Series B funding, working directly with the founding team to establish product-market fit and drive customer acquisition strategies.
```

---

### Paragraph 2: Background & Relevant Experience
Expand on their professional background, highlighting experiences most relevant to your client's needs.

**Template:**
```
[More context about their professional journey]. [Specific skills, methodologies, or experiences that align with the role]. [Additional achievements or capabilities that strengthen their candidacy].
```

**Example:**
```
Throughout her career, Sarah has specialized in taking products through critical growth phases, from initial launch through scaling to tens of millions in revenue. Her experience includes managing cross-functional teams of engineers, designers, and data analysts, driving strategic roadmaps based on customer insights, and translating complex technical capabilities into compelling product features. She has deep expertise in B2B SaaS business models, subscription pricing optimization, and building product-led growth motions.
```

---

### Paragraph 3: Motivations & Interest in This Role
Explain why they're considering a move and what specifically excites them about YOUR client.

**Template:**
```
[Why they're exploring new opportunities - always positive framing]. [Specific aspects of your client's company that appeal to them]. [How this role aligns with their career goals or aspirations].
```

**Example:**
```
Sarah is exploring new opportunities because she's seeking a role where she can have greater strategic ownership and work at an earlier stage where her product decisions have direct impact on company trajectory. She's particularly excited about your company's mission in the fintech compliance space and the opportunity to shape product direction during this critical Series A growth phase. The combination of your technical founding team, the regulatory complexity of the problem space, and the chance to build a product organization from the ground up aligns perfectly with where she wants to take her career next.
```

---

### Paragraph 4 (Optional): Additional Context or Closing
Include if there's more relevant context, or simply close with a call to action.

**Template:**
```
[Any additional relevant context]. Please let me know if you'd like to move forward with a conversation.
```

**Example:**
```
Her experience navigating complex enterprise sales cycles and working with heavily regulated industries will be particularly valuable as you scale into larger financial institutions. Please let me know if you'd like to move forward with a conversation.
```

## RED TEXT SECTION (Internal Notes for AM)

### Required Components

Use this exact format:
```
🔴 INTERNAL NOTES FOR AM - DO NOT SEND TO CLIENT

[Organized sections as shown below]
```

---

### Component 1: Visa Status
```
VISA STATUS:
- [Current work authorization status]
- [Any concerns or complications]
- [Timeline considerations]
```

**Examples:**
```
VISA STATUS:
- US Citizen - no concerns
```

```
VISA STATUS:
- Currently on H-1B with current employer
- Will need transfer (typically 2-3 week process)
- H-1B expires March 2026, renewal in process
- No cap issues, straightforward transfer expected
```

```
VISA STATUS:
- Requires H-1B sponsorship (first time, cap-subject)
- Currently on OPT, expires June 2025
- Cannot start until H-1B approval (October 2025 at earliest if selected in lottery)
- HIGH RISK - recommend discussing with client before proceeding
```

---

### Component 2: Compensation
```
COMPENSATION:
- Current: $[base] base + $[bonus] bonus + $[equity] equity = $[total] total
- Target: $[range] base, $[range] total comp
- [Additional context about flexibility, equity preferences, etc.]
- [Any concerns about alignment with client budget]
```

**Example:**
```
COMPENSATION:
- Current: $180K base + $40K bonus (80% attainment) + $80K equity/year = $300K total
- Target: $220K base minimum, $350K+ total comp preferred
- Open to strong equity package to offset lower cash
- Current equity vests through March 2026 - may want sign-on to offset forfeit
- Client's range is $200-230K base - upper end needed to be competitive
```

---

### Component 3: Competing Processes
```
COMPETING PROCESSES:
- [Company Name]: [Stage], [Timeline], [Offer expected date]
- [Company Name]: [Stage], [Timeline], [Offer expected date]
- [Ranking/Priority notes]
- [Urgency considerations]
```

**Example:**
```
COMPETING PROCESSES:
- DataCorp (direct competitor): Final round scheduled 11/12, offer expected 11/15
- StartupXYZ (Series B): Second round 11/14, offer timeline approximately 11/20-11/25
- ConsultingCo: First round next week, earlier stage
- DataCorp is currently her #1 choice (we are #2)
- URGENT: Need to move quickly to stay competitive with DataCorp timeline
```

---

### Component 4: Red Flags / Concerns
```
RED FLAGS / CONCERNS:
- [Any concerns from CLABVISSS intake]
- [Potential obstacles to closing]
- [Misalignments with role or company]
- [Behavioral or cultural concerns]
```

**Example:**
```
RED FLAGS / CONCERNS:
- Strong opinions about product strategy - may conflict with founder's vision
- Needs clear path to VP within 18 months (client should be prepared to discuss)
- Concerned about Series A stage - prefers Series B+ for stability/resources
- May require higher comp than client's budgeted range
- Has turned down 2 Series A offers in past 6 months due to stage concerns
```

---

### Component 5: Title Expectations
```
TITLE EXPECTATIONS:
- [Required title]
- [Will not accept certain titles]
- [Context or sensitivity around title]
```

**Examples:**
```
TITLE EXPECTATIONS:
- Wants "Senior Product Manager" minimum
- Will not accept "Product Manager" title (considers it a step backward)
- Open to "Lead Product Manager" as alternative
```

```
TITLE EXPECTATIONS:
- Flexible on title
- Currently "Staff Engineer" but understands startup titles differ
- More concerned with scope than title
```

---

### Component 6: Location / Work Setup
```
LOCATION / WORK SETUP:
- [Current location]
- [Commute considerations]
- [Remote/hybrid expectations]
- [Relocation willingness]
- [Any concerns]
```

**Example:**
```
LOCATION / WORK SETUP:
- Lives in Oakland, commute to SF office is 45 min (acceptable)
- Expects 3 days/week in office minimum
- Will not consider fully remote (wants in-person collaboration)
- Open to relocating to SF in 6-12 months if role goes well
```

---

### Component 7: Start Date
```
START DATE:
- [Notice period]
- [Earliest possible start]
- [Preferred timeline]
- [Any constraints or considerations]
```

**Example:**
```
START DATE:
- 3-week notice period required
- Would prefer 4 weeks to transition properly
- Earliest possible: December 1st
- Has $15K annual bonus that pays December 15th - may want to wait
- Willing to start earlier for right opportunity but would appreciate flexibility
```

---

### Component 8: Additional Context
```
ADDITIONAL CONTEXT:
- [Background verification notes]
- [Reference considerations]
- [Specific questions they have]
- [Any other relevant information]
```

**Example:**
```
ADDITIONAL CONTEXT:
- Previously worked at client's main competitor (DataCorp) - knows the market well
- Has 6-month gap in 2022 (personal sabbatical for family reasons, transparent about it)
- References are strong - former manager at DataCorp will be excellent reference
- Wants to understand client's fundraising timeline and runway (18+ months preferred)
```

---

### Component 9: Closing Strategy
```
CLOSING STRATEGY:
- [Recommendations for positioning]
- [What to emphasize]
- [How to address concerns]
- [Timeline recommendations]
```

**Example:**
```
CLOSING STRATEGY:
- Emphasize equity upside story and growth trajectory (stage concern)
- Highlight path to VP - ask client about timeline and what needs to happen
- Move quickly - DataCorp offer coming 11/15 (4 days from now)
- Consider offering flexibility on start date to preserve year-end bonus
- Schedule call with CTO to discuss technical vision (she wants to vet technical leadership)
- Prepare equity comparison model vs. DataCorp offer when it comes
```

## QUICK REFERENCE CHECKLIST

Before sending CV submission, verify:

**BLACK TEXT:**
- [ ] Subject line includes name and specific experience
- [ ] Paragraph 1: Clear intro with years and key selling points
- [ ] Paragraph 2: Relevant background and achievements
- [ ] Paragraph 3: Motivations and specific interest in THIS company
- [ ] No bullet points (full paragraphs only)
- [ ] Professional, confident, specific tone
- [ ] 300-400 words total
- [ ] Copy-paste ready (AM doesn't need to edit)

**RED TEXT:**
- [ ] Clearly separated with 🔴 header
- [ ] Visa status documented
- [ ] Compensation breakdown complete
- [ ] Competing processes listed with timelines
- [ ] Red flags and concerns noted
- [ ] Title expectations clarified
- [ ] Start date and constraints documented
- [ ] Closing strategy recommendations included

**OVERALL:**
- [ ] No placeholder [TBD] text
- [ ] All names spelled correctly
- [ ] Numbers are accurate
- [ ] Dates are correct
- [ ] Client name is correct
- [ ] No typos or grammar errors
