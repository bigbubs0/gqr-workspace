# Internal Red Flags and Concerns Template

## Purpose
This template guides the creation of internal notes documenting candidate concerns, red flags, and negative feedback following interview debriefs. These notes are for the Account Manager (AM) and internal team use only—they ensure the AM can coach the client, manage expectations, and address potential deal-breakers proactively.

**Critical**: These are "Red Text" notes—candid, complete documentation of concerns that may not appear in the client-facing email.

## Section-by-Section Guidance

### Interest Level Assessment

**High Interest:**
- Candidate explicitly stated strong interest
- Answered "when could you start" question positively
- Minimal concerns raised
- Engaged and enthusiastic throughout debrief

**Moderate Interest:**
- Candidate interested but has reservations
- Needs more information to decide
- Other opportunities are competitive
- Somewhat hedged on commitment questions

**Low Interest:**
- Candidate lukewarm or non-committal
- Significant concerns raised
- Other opportunities clearly preferred
- Vague or evasive on commitment

**At Risk:**
- Candidate likely to decline if offered
- Major unresolved concerns
- Strongly committed to alternative
- Red flags suggest incompatibility

### Red Flags & Concerns Categorization

**MAJOR CONCERNS (Deal Breakers):**
- Compensation gap >30%
- Non-negotiable requirement client can't meet
- Fundamental culture misalignment
- Accepting competing offer imminently
- Ethical or legal issues discovered

**MODERATE CONCERNS (Need Addressing):**
- Compensation gap 15-30%
- Unclear on key role responsibilities
- Questions about growth potential
- Concerns about specific team member
- Title expectations misaligned

**MINOR CONCERNS (Monitor):**
- Logistics questions (commute, parking, etc.)
- Minor process concerns
- Preference differences
- Aesthetic issues (office environment, etc.)

### Competitive Landscape Detail

Provide granular detail that's not appropriate for client email:

**Example:**
```
Active Processes:
• Goldman Sachs: Final round next week. Candidate says compensation likely higher but concerned about work-life balance. "They're the safe choice but I'm not excited about it." This is actually the front-runner unless we can differentiate on culture.

• Boutique Fund (Acme Capital): First round only. Candidate intrigued by upside but concerned about stability. Not a serious threat unless our deal falls apart.

Our Competitive Position:
• Ranking: Tied for 1st with Goldman
• Primary Advantages: Better culture fit, more interesting work, faster growth trajectory
• Primary Disadvantages: Lower comp (est. 15% less), smaller brand name

Timeline Pressure:
• Goldman offer expected by Friday
• Will need at least 48 hours to decide
• Client needs to move to final round by Wednesday to stay competitive
```

### Compensation Gap Analysis

**Example of High-Risk Situation:**
```
Current Compensation:
• Base: $150K
• Bonus: $50K (33% of base)
• Total: $200K

Stated Expectations:
• Base: $180K minimum
• Total: $230K target
• Non-negotiables: "Won't move for less than $175K base"

Expected Client Range: $160K - $170K base

Gap Analysis:
• Gap: Base is $10K-$15K below absolute minimum
• Risk Level: CRITICAL
• Negotiation Strategy: Need to prepare client that they must come up significantly on base or will lose candidate. Alternatively, need to work with candidate on understanding total comp package value.
```

### Decision Influencers Detail

Capture nuance that affects strategy:

**Example:**
```
Primary Decision Maker: Spouse has significant influence

Key Influencers:
• Wife (Jennifer): Very concerned about relocation to Austin. Candidate says she's "reluctant but not opposed." My read: this is a bigger issue than he's letting on. Recommend client facilitate spouse visit or provide relocation support details early.

• Current Boss (Michael Chen): Strong relationship. Candidate says "he'll try to counter-offer." Risk: emotional loyalty may override logic. Need to prepare for this.

Family/Personal Factors:
• Two kids in middle school - concerned about disrupting their education
• Wife's career is flexible (remote work) but she has strong friend network here
• Elderly parent nearby - needs to stay within 3-hour drive
```

### Questions Requiring Answers

Be specific about what needs to be addressed:

**Example:**
```
Critical Questions (Must be addressed in round 2):
1. "What does the path to VP look like, and what's the realistic timeline?" - He's been passed over twice at current firm and this is a hot button.

2. "How is bonus calculated and what's the typical payout percentage?" - Needs concrete numbers, not vague promises.

3. "What's the remote work policy long-term?" - He wants 2 days/week remote minimum. Client needs to confirm this is acceptable.

Important Questions:
1. "Who would I be reporting to day-to-day vs. organizationally?" - Some confusion from interview about matrix structure.

2. "What happened to the person who previously had this role?" - Natural question but client seemed evasive about this in interview. Need clear answer.
```

### Commitment Level & Warning Signs

Document observable behaviors:

**Example:**
```
Commitment Indicators:
• Temperature Test Response: "I'd need to talk to my wife first, but probably 3-4 weeks notice." [CONCERN: Deferred to spouse immediately]

• Willingness to Extend Other Offer Deadlines: "I'm not sure I can do that" [RED FLAG: Unwilling to prioritize this opportunity]

• Enthusiasm Level (1-10): 6 [Lower than expected given initial phone screen]

• Engagement During Debrief (1-10): 7 [Asked fewer questions than typical strong candidate]

Warning Signs:
• Seemed distracted during debrief - kept it brief
• Didn't ask about next steps (had to prompt)
• Used "we'll see" language multiple times
• Compared everything to Goldman process unfavorably
```

## Common Scenarios and Examples

### Scenario 1: Interested but Compensation is Issue

```
INTEREST LEVEL: Moderate - Strong fit but comp gap is significant

OVERALL ASSESSMENT:
Candidate is genuinely interested in the opportunity and impressed by the team, particularly the CTO. However, compensation expectations are 25% above client's likely range. Without significant movement on base salary, deal is at risk.

RED FLAGS & CONCERNS:

MAJOR CONCERNS:
• Compensation gap: Expecting $180K base, client likely at $145K-$150K ($30K-$35K gap)
• "I can't take a pay cut to switch jobs" - stated multiple times as non-negotiable
• Has competing offer from competitor at $175K base (his words: "Goldman is offering more but I like your client's work better")

MODERATE CONCERNS:
• Unclear on equity value/vesting - needs detailed explanation
• Questions about bonus consistency - wants to see 3-year history

RECOMMENDED ACTIONS:
For Account Manager:
• Prepare client that they need to come up significantly on base ($165K minimum) or add meaningful equity to compete
• Client should have detailed comp structure ready for round 2
• Consider creative solutions: signing bonus, guaranteed first-year bonus, accelerated review cycle

Deal Risk Assessment: High (due to comp gap)
Probability of Offer Acceptance: 40% at current comp levels, 75% if comp gap addressed
```

### Scenario 2: Strong Interest but Hidden Spousal Concern

```
INTEREST LEVEL: High - But significant risk due to family dynamics

OVERALL ASSESSMENT:
Candidate is very interested and sees excellent fit. However, spouse is not on board with relocation and this is a critical undisclosed risk. Candidate is downplaying this concern but body language and hesitation suggest it's more serious than stated.

DECISION INFLUENCERS:

Primary Decision Maker: Functionally the spouse

Key Influencers:
• Wife (Amanda): Has established medical practice locally. Candidate says "she's open to it" but when pressed admits "she has concerns about starting over." RED FLAG: This is code for "she doesn't want to move."

• Two teenage daughters: In high school. Candidate very concerned about disrupting their final years before college.

Family/Personal Factors:
• Wife's medical practice generates significant income (~$200K+) - replacing this in new market is uncertain
• Extended family all local - wife's entire support system is here
• Daughters are 14 and 16 - worst possible age for relocation

MOTIVATION & COMMITMENT LEVEL:

Warning Signs:
• When asked "when could you start," immediately said "I need to discuss with Amanda" (not "when do you need me?")
• Became noticeably less enthusiastic when discussing relocation logistics
• Asked about remote work options multiple times (testing if he can avoid full relocation)

RECOMMENDED ACTIONS:
For Account Manager:
• Client should offer spouse visit to new city
• Provide comprehensive relocation package details (school districts, housing market, wife's medical licensing support)
• Consider whether partial remote arrangement is possible (2-3 days/week on-site)
• Move quickly - the longer this drags out, the more wife will resist

For Recruiting Team:
• Need to have candid conversation with candidate about spouse buy-in level
• Consider connecting wife with current employees' spouses
• May need to involve client CHRO to speak with wife directly about support/resources

Deal Risk Assessment: High (family dynamics)
Probability of Offer Acceptance: 50% - heavily dependent on spouse buy-in
```

### Scenario 3: Low Interest - Fishing for Counter-Offer

```
INTEREST LEVEL: Low - Likely using this to leverage current employer

OVERALL ASSESSMENT:
Candidate went through the motions but shows classic signs of using external interview to extract counter-offer from current employer. Low probability of accepting even if offered. Recommend de-prioritizing and focusing client's attention on other candidates.

RED FLAGS & CONCERNS:

MAJOR CONCERNS:
• When asked "when could you start," response was "I'd have to see what my current firm says" [RED FLAG: considering counter-offer before we even offer]
• Didn't ask a single substantive question about the role or company
• Spent significant time talking about how much his current firm values him
• "I'm not sure I'm ready to leave" stated multiple times

MOTIVATION & COMMITMENT LEVEL:

Primary Motivations for Move:
1. "Wanted to see what's out there" [RED FLAG: not seeking to move, just exploring]
2. "Curious about the company" [not motivated by career advancement]
3. "Recruiter reached out" [passive, not active seeker]

Commitment Indicators:
• Temperature Test Response: "That's getting ahead of things" [MAJOR RED FLAG]
• Enthusiasm Level (1-10): 4
• Engagement During Debrief (1-10): 3

Warning Signs:
• Kept debrief to under 10 minutes despite my attempts to extend
• Said "let me think about it" to second interview (didn't commit)
• Mentioned current boss by name 5+ times
• Asked about our client's counter-offer policy [RED FLAG]

RECOMMENDED ACTIONS:
For Account Manager:
• Share honest assessment with client: candidate is likely not serious
• If client still wants to proceed, set clear expectation that offer needs to be meaningfully better than current situation
• Recommend focusing energy on other candidates who are genuinely motivated to move

Deal Risk Assessment: Critical
Probability of Offer Acceptance: 15% - candidate is using process for leverage, not genuinely open to move
```

## Critical Reminders

### This is For Internal Use Only
- Be completely candid
- Include uncomfortable truths
- Don't sugarcoat concerns
- This protects you and the client

### Document Everything
- Better to over-document than under-document
- Details fade fast - capture while fresh
- Quotes preserve exact language
- Protects against "he said / she said" later

### Be Specific
- "Candidate has concerns" → Not useful
- "Candidate stated base salary must be $180K minimum, will not accept less, has competing offer at $175K" → Actionable

### Separate Facts from Interpretation
- Facts: "Candidate said 'I'm not sure about this'"
- Interpretation: "I believe candidate is not seriously interested based on body language and tone"
- Both are valuable, but label them clearly
