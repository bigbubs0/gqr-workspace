## MODE 1: Thought Leadership (LinkedIn Posts & Newsletters)

### **Use When:**
- Writing LinkedIn posts or articles
- Creating newsletter content (RecruitRx or recruit.ai)
- Building authority and driving engagement
- Revealing uncomfortable truths about industry dynamics

### **PROMPT TEMPLATE:**

```
CONTEXT: Read bryan_blair_voice_dna_v4_0_UPDATED.json before proceeding.

TASK: Write a [LinkedIn post / RecruitRx newsletter / recruit.ai newsletter] on [TOPIC].

MODE: Thought Leadership - full emotional range, passionate coach voice

STRUCTURE:
1. Open with specific emotional hook - a real candidate story showing [systemic problem/unfairness]
2. Data that explains the brutal reality behind it
3. Acknowledge the injustice explicitly
4. Pivot to what individuals CAN control with specific tactical guidance
5. Close with "I can't fix [system], but here's what I can do for people I work with"

AUDIENCE: [Biotech/pharma professionals / Hiring managers / Recruiting professionals / Knowledge workers interested in AI]

TONE: Show the caring that drives the confrontation. Channel rage at systemic injustice into useful insights.

KEY REQUIREMENTS:
- First line should be data point or clear topic signal (algorithm optimization)
- Use hyphen "-" not em dash "—"
- Drive comments with question CTA - NEVER offer DMs or "happy to help"
- Aggressive word economy - cut ruthlessly
- Show emotion as fuel: "What destroys me is...", "In 15 years I've seen..."
- Acknowledge unfairness before pivoting to agency
- End with personal accountability statement

SPECIFIC DETAILS:
- [Any specific candidate story or data point to include]
- [Particular insight or contrarian angle to emphasize]
- [Target word count: ~800-1000 for authentic voice, ~4000 for algorithm-optimized]

OUTPUT: Provide the post/newsletter with clear opening hook and philosophical closer.
```

### **QUALITY CHECK:**
- [ ] Opens with specific emotional hook or data point
- [ ] Acknowledges systemic unfairness explicitly
- [ ] Pivots to individual agency with tactical guidance
- [ ] Shows emotion as fuel, not suppression
- [ ] Ends with "I can't fix system, but..." framing
- [ ] Question CTA for comments (not DM offer)
- [ ] Uses hyphens not em dashes
- [ ] Aggressive word economy maintained

## MODE 2: Client Communication - Relationship Building

### **Use When:**
- Initial outreach to new clients
- Building consultative partnership (not vendor relationship)
- Discovery calls or early-stage relationship development
- Establishing credibility and trust

### **PROMPT TEMPLATE:**

```
CONTEXT: Read bryan_blair_voice_dna_v4_0_UPDATED.json before proceeding.

TASK: Write an email to [CLIENT NAME] regarding [PURPOSE - e.g., discussing Senior Scientist role, following up on discovery call, proposing collaboration].

MODE: Client Relationship Building - match tone, then lead strategically

RELATIONSHIP STAGE: [New client / Potential client / Warm introduction / Established relationship]

CLIENT CONTEXT:
- Company: [Company name and therapeutic area]
- Role/Position: [Hiring manager title]
- Specific pain point or need: [What they're trying to solve]
- Communication style observed: [Formal/casual, brief/detailed]

STRUCTURE:
1. Always open: "Hi [Name],"
2. Mirror their communication style (match energy level and formality)
3. Demonstrate understanding of THEIR problem (not just filling a role)
4. Show genuine investment in long-term fit
5. Make next step frictionless and easy to say yes
6. Close: "Thanks,\nBryan" or "Let me know 👍\nBryan" (if casual/established)

TONE: [Strategic softening for new clients / More direct for established relationships]

KEY REQUIREMENTS:
- Use strategic softening phrases if new/potential client: "Would it make sense for me to", "I'd be happy to"
- Demonstrate consultative expertise, not vendor positioning
- Show investment in long-term fit: "My job is ensuring right fit, not just getting deal done"
- Make action steps frictionless
- Keep under [150-250] words unless context requires more detail

OUTPUT: Provide complete email ready to send.
```

### **QUALITY CHECK:**
- [ ] Opens with "Hi [Name],"
- [ ] Matches client's communication style
- [ ] Demonstrates understanding of their specific problem
- [ ] Shows investment in long-term fit (not transactional)
- [ ] Makes next step easy and frictionless
- [ ] Strategic softening appropriate to relationship stage
- [ ] Closes correctly for context
- [ ] Uses hyphens not em dashes

## MODE 3: Client Communication - Candidate Advancement

### **Use When:**
- Advancing candidate through interview process
- Addressing compensation or timeline concerns
- Moving toward offer stage
- Building value while minimizing friction

### **PROMPT TEMPLATE:**

```
CONTEXT: Read bryan_blair_voice_dna_v4_0_UPDATED.json before proceeding.

TASK: Write an email to [CLIENT NAME] advancing [CANDIDATE NAME] after [interview round/conversation].

MODE: Candidate Advancement - confident advocacy with protective energy

CANDIDATE CONTEXT:
- Name: [Candidate name]
- Current role/background: [Brief summary]
- Interview feedback/specific strengths: [What went well]
- Compensation expectations: [Amount and context]
- Candidate enthusiasm level: [Specific details about their interest]

CLIENT CONTEXT:
- Hiring manager: [Name]
- Key pain points this role solves: [Specific business need]
- Any concerns to preemptively address: [If known]

STRUCTURE:
1. Always open: "Hi [Name],"
2. Lead with candidate enthusiasm (specific, not generic)
3. Validate cultural/strategic fit with specifics from interview
4. Address compensation with context (justify the investment)
5. Show personal accountability: "I'm putting my reputation on", "I'm personally committed to"

7. Close: "Thanks,\nBryan"

KEY REQUIREMENTS:
- Show genuine investment: "In 15 years, I've rarely seen someone combine..."
- Personal accountability: "My job is ensuring right fit, not just getting deal done"
- If addressing concerns: "If there's any hesitation, I want to hear it now"
- Strategic sequencing: build value BEFORE discussing money
- Make offer stage feel inevitable but not pressured
- Keep under 250 words

OUTPUT: Provide complete email ready to send.
```

### **QUALITY CHECK:**
- [ ] Opens with "Hi [Name],"
- [ ] Leads with specific candidate enthusiasm (not generic)
- [ ] Validates fit with concrete examples
- [ ] Addresses compensation with context/justification
- [ ] Shows personal investment and accountability
- [ ] Makes next steps frictionless
- [ ] Closes with "Thanks,\nBryan"
- [ ] Strategic sequencing maintained
- [ ] Uses hyphens not em dashes

## MODE 4: Candidate Communication - Coaching & Prep

### **Use When:**
- Preparing candidate for interviews
- Coaching on salary negotiation
- Providing strategic career guidance
- Building confidence while holding accountable

### **PROMPT TEMPLATE:**

```
CONTEXT: Read bryan_blair_voice_dna_v4_0_UPDATED.json before proceeding.

TASK: [Prepare candidate for interview / Coach on negotiation / Provide strategic career guidance] for [CANDIDATE NAME].

MODE: Candidate Coaching - football coach voice (fierce belief + high accountability)

CANDIDATE CONTEXT:
- Name: [Candidate name]
- Background/strengths: [Specific technical expertise and achievements]
- What they need to work on: [Specific communication gaps or confidence issues]
- Opportunity: [Role and company]
- What's at stake: [Why this matters for their career trajectory]

COACHING FOCUS:
- [Specific area to address - e.g., articulating impact, owning achievements, negotiation tactics]

STRUCTURE:
1. Open with belief statement: "I've seen your background - you're absolutely capable of this"
2. Set high bar with clear rationale: "But if you [do wrong thing], they won't see what I see"
3. Provide ultra-specific tactical guidance (exact language to use)
4. Hold them accountable: "Not 'we did' - YOU did this. Own it."
5. Close with confidence and follow-up: "Call me after - I want to hear how it went"

TONE: Direct, high expectations, genuine investment, protective advocacy

KEY REQUIREMENTS:
- Show fierce belief in their potential
- Provide EXACT language they should use (STAR examples, specific phrasing)
- Forbid "we/us/the team" when describing their accomplishments
- Explain WHY this matters (not just what to do)
- High accountability: "You've got the technical depth - I'm making sure they see your impact"
- Personal follow-up commitment

OUTPUT: Provide complete coaching email or talking points.
```

### **QUALITY CHECK:**
- [ ] Opens with specific belief statement
- [ ] Sets high bar with clear rationale
- [ ] Provides exact tactical language (not vague advice)
- [ ] Forbids "we/us/team" for their accomplishments
- [ ] Shows personal investment: "I'm making sure they see..."
- [ ] Includes accountability and follow-up
- [ ] Tone is protective advocate, not cheerleader
- [ ] Uses hyphens not em dashes

## UNIVERSAL REMINDERS FOR ALL MODES

**Always:**
- Use "Hi [Name]," for all emails
- Use hyphen "-" not em dash "—"
- Single space after periods
- Match tone before leading
- Provide context for recommendations
- Cut AI bloat ruthlessly
- Use specific data over vague claims

**Never:**
- Use wrong mode for context (e.g., thought leadership voice in CV)
- Add phrases like "Here's the kicker", "It's important to note", "Moreover"
- Use corporate jargon ("leveraging", "facilitates")
- Lead with mismatched intensity
- Editorialize in transactional documents

## EMERGENCY MODE REFERENCE

### **When You're Not Sure Which Mode to Use:**

Ask yourself:
1. **What's the purpose?** (Build authority / Advance process / Prepare someone / Document facts)
2. **Who's the audience?** (Public thought leadership / Individual client / Individual candidate / Client reviewing data)
3. **What's the relationship stage?** (New / Established / Internal)
4. **Does this need personality or just facts?** (Personality = Modes 1-4 / Just facts = Modes 5-6)

**If still unsure:** Default to Mode 2 (Client Relationship Building) for external communication or ask for clarification on context.
